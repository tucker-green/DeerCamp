### 1. Stand Reservation System ü™ú

**The Problem We're Solving:**
Every club deals with this: It's 4:30am opening day, you park your truck, and realize someone else is already walking to "your" stand. The group text from last night got buried. Now what? Drive to the backup spot? Miss the morning hunt arguing? This kills the whole morning.

**What Hunters Actually Need:**

#### **A. The Stand Board - Digital Version**
Remember the old corkboard at camp with cards pinned for each stand? This is that, but it actually works.

- **Visual calendar** showing every stand, every day
- **Color-coded status**: Green (open), Red (taken), Yellow (maintenance), Orange (guest hunting)
- **Moon phase overlay** on calendar (we all plan around the moon anyway)
- **Weather forecast integration** (wind direction matters for stand selection)
- **Sunrise/sunset times** automatically calculated for your location
- **"Opening Weekend" protection** - set rules for how prime dates get allocated

#### **B. Fair Rotation System**
Because the same guys shouldn't get the honey holes every weekend.

- **Automatic rotation tracking** - "Jim had Oak Ridge last Saturday, so he's last priority this weekend"
- **Seniority points** system (if your club uses it)
- **Draw system** for prime dates (opening day, rut peak, etc.)
- **"First come first served" mode** for clubs that prefer it
- **Youth hunter priority** settings
- **Earned time** - show up for work days, get bonus booking priority

#### **C. Real-World Booking Rules**
Every club has its own culture. The system should bend to you, not the other way around.

- **24-hour advance booking minimum** (prevent morning-of grabs)
- **Maximum consecutive days** (don't be that guy)
- **Blackout dates** (nobody hunts during annual work weekend)
- **Guest booking restrictions** (members only during prime time)
- **Auto-release** (if you no-show twice, your future bookings get canceled)
- **Weather cancellation** (release stands if severe weather hits)

#### **D. The Check-In Board**
Know who's in the woods RIGHT NOW.

- **Quick check-in/check-out** (tap your stand when you arrive/leave)
- **"Currently Hunting" live dashboard** - see who's where in real-time
- **Safety zones** - shows stands that are too close to hunt simultaneously
- **Overdue alerts** - if someone doesn't check out, notify emergency contacts
- **Text notification** - "John just checked into Oak Stand" (so you don't walk that direction)

**Hunter Stories (Real Scenarios):**
- *"I'm driving to camp Friday night. I want to reserve South Ridge for Saturday morning, but if there's a north wind, I'll take Creek Bottom instead. Can I see the forecast?"*
- *"My son has his first buck tag. Can I block off the food plot stand for him opening weekend before anyone else books it?"*
- *"We've got 15 members and only 8 stands. How do we make sure everyone gets fair chances at the good spots?"*
- *"Someone left a stand reserved for 3 weeks straight but never showed up. How do we handle that?"*

### 2. Brotherhood Management üë•

**The Problem We're Solving:**
You know that Excel spreadsheet your club treasurer has? The one nobody can find? With last year's phone numbers? That gets corrupted every hunting season? Yeah, we're fixing that.

**What Makes a Hunting Club Work:**

#### **A. The Member Roster - Camp Edition**
Not a boring database. A living record of your brotherhood.

- **Member profiles** with the info that matters:
  - Hunting experience level (rookie to old-timer)
  - Preferred weapon (rifle, bow, muzzleloader, crossbow)
  - Special certifications (tracking, meat processing, guide license)
  - Emergency contact (because stuff happens)
  - Blood type (for serious emergencies)
  - Dietary restrictions (for camp meals)
  - Vehicle info (make/model/plate for gate access)
  - Tree stand safety harness certification date

- **Roles that make sense**:
  - **Camp Boss** (owns the property/lease, final say)
  - **Treasurer** (handles the money)
  - **Game Warden** (tracks harvest, reports to state)
  - **Work Captain** (organizes property maintenance)
  - **Full Member** (voting rights, full access)
  - **Associate Member** (hunt rights, limited booking)
  - **Family Member** (spouse/kids of full members)
  - **Youth Hunter** (under 18, special rules)
  - **Guest** (one-time or seasonal access)
  - **Alumni** (former members, honorary access)

#### **B. The Prospect System**
Because bringing in new blood is how camps survive.

- **Prospect application** process
  - Submit online application
  - Upload hunter safety certificate
  - Provide references (other members vouch for you)
  - Pay application fee (covers background check)

- **Guest hunt trial period**
  - Let prospects hunt 1-3 times before voting them in
  - Track their behavior (leave stands clean? Follow rules? Safe?)
  - Existing members can leave feedback

- **Voting system**
  - Full members vote on new members
  - Can require unanimous, 2/3 majority, or simple majority
  - Anonymous voting option
  - Blackball provision (if your club uses it)

- **Onboarding checklist for new members**:
  - [ ] Pay initiation fee
  - [ ] Pay dues
  - [ ] Sign liability waiver
  - [ ] Complete property orientation
  - [ ] Meet all current members
  - [ ] Learn stand locations
  - [ ] Review club rules
  - [ ] Get gate code/key
  - [ ] Add to emergency contact list

#### **C. Dues & Finances**
Nobody likes chasing down money.

- **Automatic dues reminders** (email/text before due date)
- **Online payment** (credit card, bank transfer, Venmo, PayPal)
- **Payment plans** (split annual dues into monthly payments)
- **Late fee automation** (don't make the treasurer be the bad guy)
- **Work day credits** (reduce dues if you show up for maintenance)
- **Per-harvest fees** (if your club charges them)
- **Guest fees** (daily/weekend/seasonal rates)
- **Equipment damage deposits**
- **Expense reimbursements** (bought mineral for the food plot? Submit receipt)
- **Budget tracking** (lease payment, insurance, fuel, seed, feed)
- **Financial reports** (treasurer can pull P&L anytime)
- **Tax documents** (generate 1099s if needed for contractors)

#### **D. The Invitation System**
Word-of-mouth is how good camps grow.

- **Referral tracking** (who brought who in - matters for vouching)
- **Guest pass generation** (send friend a day pass with QR code)
- **Family sharing** (members can invite spouse/kids easily)
- **Corporate groups** (some camps host business clients)
- **Charity hunts** (veteran's day, kids with cancer, etc.)

**Hunter Stories:**
- *"My buddy wants to join. How do I get him on the list for next year's member vote?"*
- *"Three guys haven't paid dues since last season. Can I auto-suspend their booking rights until they pay?"*
- *"My son just turned 16 and got his license. How do I add him as a youth member?"*
- *"We want to do a Heroes Hunt for disabled vets next spring. How do I track those guest hunts separately?"*

### 3. The Compound Map üó∫Ô∏è

**The Problem We're Solving:**
New member shows up opening morning. "Where's the Oak Stand?" Nobody's up to show them. They wander around in the dark. Spook deer. Get lost. Everyone's pissed.

**What a Hunting Map Needs to Be:**

#### **A. Property Intelligence**
This isn't just a map. It's institutional knowledge.

- **Satellite view with property boundaries** (no trespassing on accident)
- **Every stand marked with custom pins**:
  - Stand name
  - Stand type (ladder, climber, blind, box, ground)
  - Height
  - Capacity (1-person or 2-person)
  - Condition (good, needs repair, unsafe)
  - Last maintenance date
  - Which direction it faces
  - Best wind directions for that stand
  - Photo of the stand
  - Photo of the shooting lanes
  - Photo of the view from the stand

- **Access routes marked**:
  - Driving routes (which stands you can drive to)
  - Walking trails (blazed paths)
  - Quiet approaches (how to sneak in without spooking deer)
  - ATV trails
  - Seasonal routes (creek crossings in dry season only)

- **Food plots mapped**:
  - Plot name
  - Acreage
  - What's planted
  - When it was planted
  - Watering schedule
  - Expected maturity date
  - Photos of growth progress

- **Key terrain features**:
  - Water sources (creeks, ponds, springs)
  - Bedding areas (don't walk through these!)
  - Oak groves (acorn crops)
  - Thickets
  - Ridge lines
  - Funnels and pinch points
  - Scrape areas (deer sign)
  - Rub lines
  - Historical kill zones ("big buck was shot here last year")

#### **B. Layered Intel**
Turn it on and off like onX Hunt.

- **Wind overlay** (current wind direction, see if your stand is viable)
- **Topographic lines** (elevation matters)
- **Timber type** (hardwood vs pine)
- **Distance rings** (200, 300, 400 yards from stands)
- **Shot distance markers** (known yardages to shooting lanes)
- **Trail camera locations**
- **Mineral station locations**
- **Feeder locations** (where legal)
- **Property line buffer zones** (how close to edges)
- **Private land boundaries** (neighbor's property)

#### **C. Offline Works**
Because cell service in the woods is trash.

- **Download maps for offline use**
- **GPS still works without cell service**
- **Share offline map packages** (send to new members)
- **Print full-size maps** (laminate them for the camp wall)

#### **D. Smart Features**
The stuff that makes you look like a pro.

- **"Show me the best stand for a NW wind"** - auto-filters stands
- **"Which stands can I drive to?"** - highlights access
- **"Where are the closest two stands to Oak Ridge?"** - distance calculator
- **"Show me everywhere we've seen big bucks"** - heat map of sightings
- **Measure distance** - tap two points, get yardage
- **Record tracks** - GPS tracks your walk in/out (learn the quiet routes)

**Hunter Stories:**
- *"I've got a west wind tomorrow morning. Which stand should I hunt?"*
- *"New guy is hunting South Stand. How do I show him the walking trail so he doesn't drive the truck back there and spook everything?"*
- *"We need to add the new box blind we built last month."*
- *"Where exactly was that rub line we found during shed season?"*

### 4. Trophy Book - Digital Edition üìñ

**The Problem We're Solving:**
The old paper logbook disappeared. Again. Nobody knows if this is a club record. There's a blurry photo on someone's phone from 3 years ago but nobody can find it. History is getting lost.

**What the Harvest Log Should Be:**

#### **A. The Full Story**
Every harvest deserves to be remembered right.

- **Multiple high-res photos**:
  - Field photo (with hunter)
  - Antler close-up
  - Full body shot
  - Unique characteristics (drop tine, sticker point, scars)
  - Butcher shop photo (meat yield)

- **Complete measurements**:
  - **For bucks**: Points, main beam length, inside spread, gross score, net score
  - **Scoring systems**: Boone & Crockett, Pope & Young, SCI
  - **Field data**: Live weight, dressed weight, estimated age
  - **Body measurements**: Chest girth, body length, antler mass

- **The hunt details** (the story):
  - Which stand
  - Time of day
  - Weather (temp, wind, barometric pressure)
  - Moon phase
  - What you saw before the shot
  - Distance of shot
  - Shot placement
  - Blood trail notes (for teaching others)
  - Time from shot to recovery
  - How far the deer ran
  - Who helped recover/process
  - Personal notes (what you learned, memorable moments)

#### **B. Species-Specific Tracking**

**Deer (Whitetail, Mule Deer, Blacktail)**:
- Age estimate (teeth, body characteristics)
- Antler configuration (typical vs. non-typical)
- Rut activity (chasing, cruising, standing)
- Doe with fawns?

**Turkey**:
- Subspecies (Eastern, Merriam's, Rio Grande, Osceola)
- Beard length (multiple beards tracked)
- Spur length
- Weight
- Time of season (early vs. late)
- Call sequence that worked
- Gobbling activity

**Hogs**:
- Weight class
- Tusk measurements
- Sounder size (how many total)
- Damage they were causing

**Predators** (where legal):
- Coyote/bobcat/fox
- Why they were taken (livestock protection, population control)
- Pelt quality

**Waterfowl** (if club does duck hunts):
- Species ID
- Band recovery (report to USGS automatically)
- Location of harvest

#### **C. Club Records & Recognition**

- **Automatic record tracking**:
  - Biggest buck (by score)
  - Heaviest deer (by weight)
  - First harvest of season
  - Last harvest of season
  - Oldest buck (by age)
  - Most points
  - Longest beard (turkey)
  - Heaviest hog
  - Records by weapon type (bow, rifle, muzzleloader)
  - Records by member (personal bests)
  - Records by stand (which produces the most)
  - Records by season (compare years)

- **Trophy wall (virtual)**:
  - Hall of Fame section
  - "Wall of Giants" (all club record holders)
  - "Legends" (historic harvests)
  - Can print/export for physical trophy wall

#### **D. Tag & Permit Compliance**

- **Track tags used** (know when you've filled out)
- **Bag limit warnings** ("You've taken 2 does, you have 1 remaining")
- **Season dates** (know when you can legally hunt)
- **Reporting requirements** (some states require online reporting)
- **Export harvest reports** for state wildlife agencies
- **QR code scanning** (scan your physical tag, auto-links to harvest)

#### **E. Harvest Analytics** (for management)

- **Herd health indicators**:
  - Sex ratio (bucks vs does harvested)
  - Age structure (too many young bucks? good sign)
  - Body weights trending up or down
  - Antler quality improving or declining

- **Harvest pressure maps**:
  - Which stands produce the most harvests
  - Which areas are under-hunted
  - When most deer are taken (time of day heatmap)

- **Success rates**:
  - Member success percentage
  - Stand success percentage
  - Weapon-type success rates
  - Time-of-season success rates

**Hunter Stories:**
- *"I just shot a 9-pointer. I want to log it with measurements and photos before we field dress it."*
- *"Is this the biggest 8-point ever taken from Oak Stand?"*
- *"We're trying to figure out why nobody sees deer from North Ridge anymore. Can we look at historical harvest data?"*
- *"My grandson just shot his first deer. I want to add him to the Youth Hunter Hall of Fame."*
- *"State wildlife officer wants to see our harvest records from the last 3 years."*

### 5. Safety First - Brotherhood Always üö®

**The Problem We're Solving:**
Every year, hunters get hurt. Falls from stands. Heart attacks in the woods. Lost in the dark. Hypothermia. We all know someone who's had a close call. Technology should help prevent tragedies.

**What Real Safety Looks Like:**

#### **A. Who's in the Woods Right Now**

- **Live check-in board**:
  - Quick tap to check in when you arrive at your stand
  - Choose your stand from the map
  - System timestamps your check-in
  - Shows all other hunters currently checked in
  - Shows which stands are occupied

- **Safety zone violations**:
  - If two hunters check into stands less than 300 yards apart, alert both
  - "John is hunting Creek Stand. That's 250 yards from you."
  - Prevents crossfire situations
  - Adjustable safety distance by club preference

- **Overdue alerts**:
  - Set expected return time ("I'll be out by 11am")
  - If you don't check out by your time, system alerts emergency contacts
  - Escalating notifications (text, then call, then 911 if no response)
  - "Buddy check" - system asks another member to go check on you

#### **B. Emergency Response**

- **Panic button**:
  - One-tap SOS alert
  - Instantly notifies all club members
  - Shows your EXACT GPS location
  - Calls 911 automatically (in supported areas)
  - "Jim hit the panic button at Oak Stand - coordinates sent to your phone"

- **Medical info on file**:
  - Blood type (for serious trauma)
  - Allergies (medication allergies)
  - Pre-existing conditions (diabetes, heart conditions, etc.)
  - Current medications
  - Doctor contact info
  - Insurance info (for ER)

- **Emergency contact chain**:
  - Primary: Spouse/family
  - Secondary: Hunting buddy
  - Tertiary: Club president
  - Final: 911

- **AED locations** (if club has them)
  - Mark on map
  - Check battery status reminders
  - Training video access

#### **C. Weather Warnings**

- **Severe weather alerts**:
  - Lightning detected within 10 miles - alert all checked-in hunters
  - Tornado warning - immediate evacuation notice
  - Temperature danger (hypothermia risk or heat stroke risk)
  - Wind speed warnings (falling branches, climbing stands unsafe)

- **Shooting hours**:
  - Auto-calculate legal shooting times for your location
  - Push notifications: "Legal shooting light in 15 minutes"
  - Countdown timer to sunrise/sunset
  - Moon rise/set times (for those of us who hunt transitions)

#### **D. Tree Stand Safety**

- **Harness check reminders**:
  - "Last harness inspection was 6 months ago - inspect before hunting"
  - Log annual safety inspections
  - Track harness expiration dates (they do expire)

- **Stand maintenance tracking**:
  - Mark stands as "needs repair"
  - Block booking on unsafe stands
  - Work order system to fix them
  - Photo documentation of issues

- **Youth hunter requirements**:
  - Must hunt with adult
  - Can't book stands alone
  - Special safety briefing required
  - Parent/guardian check-in required

#### **E. Club-Specific Safety Rules**

Because every property has unique hazards.

- **Hazard warnings**:
  - "Steep ravine north of this stand - stay on trail"
  - "Barbed wire fence 100 yards east"
  - "Old well pit in this area - marked with flag"
  - "Bee hive in tree near this stand"
  - "Aggressive hogs spotted in this area"

- **First-timer requirements**:
  - New members must complete property orientation
  - Must hunt with veteran member first time
  - Quiz on safety rules before first solo hunt

- **Equipment requirements**:
  - Must use safety harness in all elevated stands
  - Must carry two-way radio or cell phone
  - Must wear orange (where required by state law)
  - Weapon safety check at check-in

**Hunter Stories:**
- *"I'm having chest pains in the stand. I need help NOW."*
- *"It's been dark for an hour. Jim hasn't checked out from his stand and he's not answering his phone."*
- *"Lightning just lit up the sky. I need to know if anyone else is still out here so we can all get to shelter."*
- *"The ladder on South Stand has a cracked rung. Nobody should hunt that until we fix it."*
- *"First-time guest is going out tomorrow morning. He needs to know about that washout on the trail."*

### 9. Scouting & Intelligence Network üîç

**The Problem We're Solving:**
Knowledge is power. One guy sees a giant buck on trail cam. Another guy finds a scrape line. Someone else patterns the does feeding in the beans. But nobody's connecting the dots. We need to share intel like a tactical unit.

**What Real Scouting Looks Like:**

#### **A. Trail Camera Central**

Trail cams are our eyes in the woods 24/7. Make them actually useful.

- **Upload trail cam photos**:
  - Direct upload from SD card or phone app
  - Auto-read EXIF data (date, time, camera location)
  - Organize by camera location
  - Tag date/time automatically

- **Mark on the map**:
  - Every camera pinned to exact GPS location
  - Click camera marker to see its recent photos
  - Know which cameras haven't been checked recently
  - Battery change schedule reminders

- **AI photo tagging** (this is the future):
  - Auto-detect: Deer, buck, doe, fawn, turkey, hog, coyote, etc.
  - Count points on bucks
  - Estimate age class
  - Track individual bucks over time (pattern recognition)
  - "This is the same 8-point from Camera 3 last week"

- **Activity analysis**:
  - Peak movement times per camera
  - Moon phase correlation
  - Weather pattern correlation
  - "This camera shows most activity between 6:30-8:00am"

- **Share the best ones**:
  - Post highlight photos to camp feed
  - "Check out this bruiser from Camera 4 last night!"
  - Generate excitement for upcoming hunts

#### **B. Deer Movement Intel**

Connect the sightings to understand patterns.

- **Sighting reports** (even if you don't shoot):
  - "Saw 3 does and a 6-point at 7:15am from Oak Stand"
  - Log every deer you see, not just harvests
  - Direction of travel
  - What they were doing (feeding, bedding, cruising)
  - Which way they came from / went to

- **Pattern mapping**:
  - System plots all sightings on map
  - Heat map shows where deer are being seen most
  - Travel corridor identification
  - "Deer are moving from bedding area to food plot via this ridge"

- **Behavioral tracking**:
  - **Pre-rut**: Bucks in bachelor groups
  - **Seeking phase**: Bucks cruising alone
  - **Chasing phase**: Bucks running does
  - **Lockdown**: Bucks with does, hard to pattern
  - **Post-rut**: Bucks back on food sources

- **Rut indicators**:
  - Scrape locations marked on map
  - Rub lines documented with photos
  - "Active scrape" status tracking
  - Primary vs. secondary scrapes
  - Community scrapes (the big ones all bucks hit)

#### **C. Sign Documentation**

Physical evidence in the woods.

- **Tracks**:
  - Photo with ruler for scale
  - GPS pin location
  - Estimated size (direction of travel)
  - Fresh vs. old
  - "Found a monster track headed toward Creek Bottom"

- **Rubs**:
  - Photo from multiple angles
  - Tree diameter (bigger trees = bigger bucks)
  - Height of rub (shoulder height indicator)
  - Freshness
  - Direction of travel from rub pattern

- **Scrapes**:
  - Photo of scrape
  - Diameter measurement
  - Active or old
  - Licking branch condition
  - "Freshened up daily" vs. "hit occasionally"

- **Bedding areas**:
  - Document but don't over-pressure
  - Mark on map with "Do not disturb" zone
  - Approach routes that avoid bedding

- **Trails**:
  - Main trails vs. secondary trails
  - Width (more traffic = wider)
  - Fresh tracks = active trail
  - Connect bedding to food sources

#### **D. Food Source Tracking**

Deer gotta eat. Track what's hot.

- **Natural food sources**:
  - Oak tree locations (mark each tree)
  - Acorn drop timing (which trees dropping first)
  - Persimmon trees
  - Apple trees
  - Honeysuckle patches
  - Browse plants
  - "White oaks on south ridge just started dropping - hunt there!"

- **Ag field monitoring** (if you border farms):
  - What's planted (corn, beans, wheat)
  - When it's harvested
  - Deer feeding patterns in fields
  - "Beans are turning yellow - deer will be hammering them"

- **Food plot intel**:
  - Which plots deer are using
  - Time of day they're feeding
  - How many deer per evening
  - "North plot: 12 deer last 3 evenings from 6-7pm"

- **Seasonal shifts**:
  - Early season: Soybeans and soft mast
  - Mid season: Acorns and food plots
  - Late season: Standing corn and brassicas
  - Track the transitions

#### **E. Weather & Moon Intelligence**

Because conditions matter as much as location.

- **Barometric pressure tracking**:
  - Pressure drops = increased movement
  - Stable high pressure = daytime bedding
  - "Barometer dropping 0.2" today - expect afternoon action"

- **Cold front alerts**:
  - "Cold front moving in tonight - tomorrow morning will be hot!"
  - Temperature drops = increased feeding
  - First cold snaps of fall = best hunting

- **Wind analysis**:
  - Track which stands are huntable in which wind directions
  - "NW wind - hunt Creek Stand or South Ridge"
  - Thermal current notes (morning vs evening)

- **Moon phase correlation**:
  - Track harvest success by moon phase
  - Overhead/underfoot times
  - "Last 3 years, most bucks harvested during waning crescent"
  - Some guys swear by it, some don't - track your own data

- **Precipitation impact**:
  - Before rain: Increased movement
  - During rain: Bedded unless heavy rut
  - After rain: Great tracking conditions, fresh sign visible
  - Snow: Jackpot for tracking, see exactly where they're moving

#### **F. Shared Hunting Calendar**

Beyond just booking stands - share your plans.

- **Hunt plan sharing** (optional):
  - "I'm hunting Oak Stand Saturday morning, then moving to Creek Bottom in the evening"
  - Helps others plan around you
  - Can coordinate drives or pushes

- **Group hunt coordination**:
  - Organize doe management drives
  - Push troublesome areas
  - Turkey hunting pairs
  - "Need 4 guys for a hog hunt Saturday - who's in?"

- **Off-property opportunities**:
  - "Public land opener - who wants to team up?"
  - "Out-of-state trip planning"
  - "Turkey hunting in Missouri - got extra spots"

**Hunter Stories:**
- *"Found a massive scrape next to the oak grove. Marked it on the map. Everyone should check it out."*
- *"Trail cam photos show the same big 10-point hitting Camera 4 every morning at 7:20am this week."*
- *"White oaks on north ridge are dropping HEAVY. That's where I'd hunt this weekend."*
- *"Barometer is dropping tonight. Cold front tomorrow. I'm taking the day off work to hunt."*
- *"We've got 4 different sightings of a big 8-point in the south end. Let's compare notes and figure out his pattern."*

### 11. Property Management Command Center üå≤

**The Problem We're Solving:**
Managing hunting property is a second job. Stands need repair. Food plots need planting. Fences need fixing. Gates need new locks. Equipment breaks. Nobody remembers when we last limed the soil or cleared the shooting lanes. It's chaos.

**What Property Management Needs:**

#### **A. Stand & Blind Management**

Every stand is an investment. Track them like assets.

**Stand Inventory:**

- **Complete stand database**:
  - Stand name/number
  - Type (ladder, hang-on, climber, box blind, ground blind, shooting house)
  - GPS coordinates
  - Photo of stand
  - Date installed
  - Who built it
  - Construction materials used
  - Capacity (1 or 2 person)
  - Height
  - Shooting directions
  - Best wind directions
  - Access route notes

- **Condition tracking**:
  - Current status (Good, Fair, Needs Repair, Unsafe)
  - Last inspection date
  - Inspection checklist:
    - [ ] Ladder rungs secure
    - [ ] Platform solid
    - [ ] Safety rails intact
    - [ ] Seat condition
    - [ ] Shooting rails functional
    - [ ] No wasp nests
    - [ ] Clear shooting lanes
    - [ ] Quiet approach maintained
  - Photos of any damage
  - Repair priority (Critical, High, Medium, Low)

- **Maintenance schedule**:
  - Annual inspection reminder
  - Painting schedule (treat wood every 2-3 years)
  - Bolt tightening schedule
  - Shooting lane clearing (2x per year)
  - Scent elimination (spray down before season)

- **Repair work orders**:
  - Create ticket: "South Stand ladder rung cracked"
  - Assign to member(s)
  - Materials needed list
  - Mark stand as "Unsafe - Do Not Hunt" until fixed
  - Photo proof of repair completion
  - Close ticket when done

**Stand Logs:**

- **Usage tracking**: How many hunts per stand per season
- **Success rate**: Harvests vs. total hunts
- **Productivity trends**: Is this stand declining in effectiveness?
